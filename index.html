Atlantic City Maintains Momentum Through Weekend Activity as New Week Brings Continued Growth and Events

By Zachary J. Blum — BlackSheep Patriots News

Atlantic City carried strong momentum through the weekend, with steady tourism, active nightlife, and consistent foot traffic across the Boardwalk and casino districts. Visitors filled major resorts and entertainment venues, signaling early-season growth as the city moves toward peak summer months.

Law enforcement maintained a visible presence in high-traffic areas, focusing on crowd management and prevention, with only minor incidents reported. Weather played a moderate role, with coastal winds and cooler evenings shifting activity indoors while daytime conditions supported Boardwalk and beach traffic.

Dining demand remained strong at established venues, reinforcing Atlantic City’s reputation as a regional food destination. Looking ahead, improving weather, scheduled events, and steady casino traffic are expected to sustain growth into the week.

Source: Regional reporting supported by The Press of Atlantic City and local data.

Culture & Commentary

Becoming Me: Cultural and Socialization in My Life

By Zachary J. Blum — Personal Essay

Three main forces shaped who I am today: my family, the streets, and the corrections system. Each one taught me different rules, work ethics, survival strategies, and how to handle life with what you have.

Family was my foundation. It is where I learned right from wrong, responsibility, and independence. The streets became my second teacher, shaping toughness, awareness, and grit.

The corrections system became a turning point. It forced reflection, discipline, and self-control. What felt like confinement became transformation. My past did not break me — it built me.
